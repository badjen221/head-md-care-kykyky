# KyKyKy


## Context


This project was created as part of an exhibition exploring seven communities and contexts of cohabitation. Each student group chose one context and developed a poetic form to tell their story.


**Our context: Cohabitation**


---


## Story


Seen through baby Rokhy's eyes, *KyKyKy* follows the first three years of her life in a shared apartment with two unexpected roommates:
**Lucky**, a warm and devoted English Bulldog, and **Cheeky**, a grumpy Maine Coon cat who wants nothing to do with anyone.


Rokhy and Lucky are born on the same day in neighboring apartments and quickly become inseparable Lucky rocks her, sleeps beside her, watches over her. Life is gentle and quiet.


Then Cheeky moves in. She claims the robot cleaner as her throne, steals Lucky's food, and pointedly ignores Rokhy's complaints. The tension between the three cohabitants slowly rises...


Until Lucky falls ill. And everything changes.


Together, Rokhy, Lucky, and Cheeky become the **KyKyKy** team and discover that living together means learning to care for one another.


---


##  Intention


In a world where cohabitation is often chosen out of necessity rather than desire, our story follows baby Rokhy, a dog, and a cat who are thrown together without asking — each with their own needs, rhythms, and stubbornness. The central dilemma is not conflict itself, but the silent question it raises: *does sharing a space oblige us to share care?* Through Rokhy's wide, uncomplicated gaze, we explore how tenderness can emerge from friction, and how the act of noticing someone's vulnerability even someone who annoys you is the first step toward belonging together.

##  Personas


| Character | Role | What they want | What they need |
|-----------|------|----------------|----------------|
| **Rokhy** | Baby | To understand the world around her | Connection, safety, wonder |
| **Lucky** | English Bulldog | To love and protect | To be cared for in return |
| **Cheeky** | Maine Coon cat | To be left alone | To belong, without admitting it |
